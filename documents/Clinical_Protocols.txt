Clinical Protocols
PUBLIC (Nurse + Doctor + Admin)
Emergency Department – Initial Stabilization
In Emergency Department – Initial Stabilization, all patients must undergo systematic assessment
and continuous monitoring. Clinical staff must follow standardized protocols to ensure patient safety
and early detection of deterioration.
Vital signs including temperature, pulse, respiratory rate, blood pressure, and oxygen saturation
must be recorded at defined intervals based on patient condition.
Nursing staff must ensure proper documentation, timely medication administration, and adherence
to hygiene protocols.
Patient comfort, positioning, hydration, and nutrition must be maintained consistently.
Early warning signs such as sudden drop in oxygen saturation, altered consciousness, or abnormal
vitals must be escalated immediately to senior staff.
All procedures must follow infection control protocols including hand hygiene, PPE usage, and
sterile techniques where required.

Emergency Advanced Management
Emergency Advanced Management involves comprehensive clinical evaluation, interpretation of
investigations, and evidence-based decision making.
Physicians must assess patient history, perform physical examination, and correlate findings with
laboratory and imaging results.
Treatment plans must be individualized considering patient comorbidities, severity of condition, and
response to therapy.
Escalation protocols must be followed in cases of deterioration, including ICU transfer or advanced
interventions.
Drug selection must consider contraindications, interactions, and patient-specific factors.
Continuous reassessment is essential to modify treatment plans and improve patient outcomes.
Clinical decisions must be documented clearly with rationale and expected outcomes.
