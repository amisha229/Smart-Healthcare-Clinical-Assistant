Treatment Guidelines
PUBLIC (Nurse + Doctor + Admin)
Emergency Care
In Emergency Care, all patients must undergo systematic assessment including initial triage,
stabilization, and continuous monitoring.
Vital parameters such as temperature, pulse, blood pressure, respiratory rate, and oxygen
saturation must be recorded and tracked over time.
Nursing care must ensure medication adherence, hygiene, patient comfort, and accurate
documentation.
Early identification of deterioration including abnormal vitals, altered consciousness, or respiratory
distress must trigger escalation.
Supportive care includes hydration, nutrition, positioning, and symptomatic relief.
All procedures must follow strict infection control and safety protocols.
Communication between healthcare providers must be timely and accurate to ensure continuity of
care.

Emergency Advanced Management
Emergency Advanced Management requires detailed clinical reasoning, evidence-based decision
making, and individualized patient management.
Comprehensive history taking and physical examination must guide diagnostic planning.
Investigations including laboratory tests and imaging must be interpreted in clinical context.
Treatment protocols must consider severity, comorbidities, and patient-specific risks.
Drug therapy must include correct selection, dosing, monitoring for side effects, and adjustments.
Escalation strategies must be clearly defined for deterioration including ICU transfer or advanced
interventions.
Continuous reassessment must be performed to evaluate response to treatment.
Clinical documentation must include rationale for decisions and expected outcomes.
